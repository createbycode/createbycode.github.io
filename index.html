Introducing micro:bit classes

A micro:bit is a half credit card sized programmable computer with 2 buttons and 25 LEDs. It has bluetooth, accelerometer, compass and 19 ways to connect to the outside world. It is playable out of the box and is designed by BBC to teach computer science in the UK.

In this course, kids will be taught programming using block based programming. The code they write in an online IDE will be downloaded into their personal micro:bit and will run on it! In case you do not want to buy a micro:bit, the online IDE also has a virtual micro:bit to try the code.

It will be roughly a 3 month course, with a personal project at the end. In the project, kids will chose what they want to build and they will build it on their own under some guidance and together with other kids. You can check out some of the cool projects here to give you an idea of what is possible.

Our Mission

To make children more creative and confident. Objective is to impart knowledge and challenge them in a safe environment. Key result is when they know basics so well that they can program almost anything independently.

There are many programs which teach programming today but they have 2 typical problems: (1) they are canned and treat all kids the same and (2) most of them are in a hurry.

My idea is to put the fundamentals in place, even if it means that we take some extra time. But when we reach there, we reach there in style!
